Common Dodge & Ram Issues We Fix

HEMI Tick & Lifter Failure

The distinctive "HEMI tick" in 5.7L and 6.4L engines is often caused by worn lifters or a collapsed lifter roller. Left unchecked, it can damage the camshaft. We diagnose the source of the noise and replace failed lifters to restore quiet, reliable operation.

Transmission Issues (845RE / ZF 8-Speed)

The 845RE and ZF 8-speed automatics used in many Dodge and Ram vehicles are known for harsh shifting, delayed engagement, and torque converter shudder. We perform fluid flushes, solenoid replacement, and complete transmission rebuilds as needed.

TIPM Module Failure

The Totally Integrated Power Module (TIPM) controls fuel pump relays, wipers, horn, and other electrical functions. Faulty TIPMs can cause no-start conditions, random warning lights, and intermittent electrical gremlins. We test and replace failed TIPM units.

Exhaust Manifold Bolt Breakage

Broken exhaust manifold bolts are extremely common on HEMI engines, causing an exhaust leak and a ticking noise especially during cold starts. We extract broken bolts and replace gaskets to eliminate the leak and restore proper exhaust flow.

Ball Joint Wear (Ram Trucks)

Ram trucks, especially 2500 and 3500 models, put heavy loads on front suspension ball joints. Worn ball joints cause clunking, wandering steering, and uneven tire wear. We inspect, press, and replace ball joints to keep your truck tracking straight and safe.

Cylinder Deactivation Problems (MDS)

The Multi-Displacement System (MDS) in HEMI engines can develop issues with the solenoids and lifters that control cylinder deactivation, leading to misfires, rough idle, and reduced fuel economy. We diagnose MDS-related faults and repair or disable the system as needed.
