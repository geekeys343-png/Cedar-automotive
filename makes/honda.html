Common Honda Issues We Fix

CVT Transmission Issues

Honda's CVT transmissions can develop shuddering, hesitation, and slipping — especially in Civic and HR-V models. We perform CVT fluid changes and diagnose internal wear before problems escalate to costly rebuilds.

AC Compressor Failure

Honda Civics and Accords are prone to AC compressor clutch failure and refrigerant leaks. If your AC is blowing warm air or making a clicking noise, we can diagnose and repair the system to keep you cool through Iowa summers.

VTC Actuator Rattle

A cold-start rattle in many Honda 4-cylinder engines (Civic, Accord, CR-V) is often caused by a worn VTC actuator. Left unaddressed, it can affect engine timing and performance. We replace the actuator and restore quiet operation.

Paint & Clear Coat Peeling

While not a mechanical repair, many Honda owners notice premature clear coat failure. We can point you in the right direction for body work, and we make sure the mechanical side of your Honda is in top shape.

Starter Issues

Honda starters, particularly in the Accord and CR-V, can fail prematurely. Symptoms include slow cranking, clicking sounds, or no-start conditions. We test and replace starters to get you back on the road fast.

Power Steering Pump Whine

Older Honda models with hydraulic power steering are known for pump noise and leaks. We diagnose power steering system issues, replace worn pumps, and flush the fluid to restore smooth, quiet steering.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I service my Honda CVT transmission?

Honda recommends CVT fluid changes every 30,000 to 60,000 miles depending on driving conditions. If you notice shuddering, slipping, or delayed engagement, bring your Honda to Cedar Automotive for a CVT inspection right away.

Does Cedar Automotive work on Honda hybrid vehicles?

Yes. We service Honda hybrid models including the Civic Hybrid, Accord Hybrid, CR-V Hybrid, and Insight. Our technicians are equipped to handle hybrid-specific systems including the IMA and two-motor hybrid systems.

When does my Honda need a timing belt replacement?

Honda models with timing belts (older Accords, Odysseys, Pilots, and Ridgelines with the V6 engine) typically need replacement between 60,000 and 105,000 miles. Newer Honda models use timing chains that generally last the life of the engine but should still be inspected if you hear rattling on cold starts.

Do you program Honda keys and fobs?

Yes — we program Honda keys and key fobs for all models including Civic, Accord, CR-V, Pilot, and Odyssey. We handle transponder key cutting, smart key fob programming, and all-keys-lost situations at independent shop prices. Call (319) 450-7584 for a quote.
