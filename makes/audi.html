Common Audi Issues We Repair

Excessive Oil Consumption

Many Audi 2.0T TSI and TFSI engines from 2009 to 2017 are known for burning oil between changes. This is often caused by faulty piston rings or PCV valve failures. We diagnose the root cause and recommend the most cost-effective repair, whether that means replacing the PCV valve assembly or addressing internal engine wear.

Timing Chain Tensioner Failure

The early 2.0T engines (EA888 Gen 1 and Gen 2) are notorious for timing chain tensioner failures, which can lead to chain skip and catastrophic engine damage. Warning signs include a rattle at cold startup. We inspect and replace the tensioner, chain, and guides with updated revision parts before failure occurs.

Turbo Wastegate Rattle

The IHI turbocharger found in many 2.0T Audis commonly develops a wastegate rattle caused by wear in the actuator arm linkage. While the rattle alone may not affect performance immediately, it can worsen and eventually trigger fault codes. We repair or replace the wastegate actuator and turbo assembly as needed.

Electrical & Module Faults

Audi vehicles use dozens of networked control modules communicating over CAN bus. Convenience module failures, window regulator issues, MMI screen glitches, and parasitic battery drains are all common. Our dealer-level scanner identifies the failing module and communication faults that basic code readers miss.

Water Pump & Thermostat Failure

Audi 2.0T engines use a plastic-impeller water pump that frequently fails between 60,000 and 100,000 miles, leading to coolant leaks or overheating. We replace the water pump and thermostat together as a preventive measure, since both components share the same labor and commonly fail in the same mileage window.

Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves

Direct-injection Audi engines (TSI/TFSI) do not wash the intake valves with fuel the way port-injection engines do. Over time, carbon deposits build up on the valve faces, causing rough idle, misfires, and reduced power. We perform walnut blasting to clean the intake valves and restore proper engine performance.

Audi Service FAQ

Do you have the right diagnostic tools for Audi vehicles?

Yes. Cedar Automotive carries dealer-level diagnostic equipment specifically designed for Audi and the broader VAG platform. We can read manufacturer-specific fault codes, run guided fault-finding procedures, perform coding and adaptations, and access systems like MMI, Quattro, and DSG that generic scan tools cannot reach.

How often does my Audi need an oil change?

Most modern Audi TSI and TFSI engines use a long-life service interval that can stretch to 10,000 miles, but we recommend changing your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles — especially in Iowa's temperature extremes. Audi engines run tight tolerances and are prone to oil consumption, so shorter intervals help protect against premature wear. We use Audi-approved VW 502/504 specification oil.

Is it cheaper to service my Audi at an independent shop vs. the dealership?

In most cases, yes. Cedar Automotive uses the same dealer-level diagnostic tools and OEM-quality parts, but our labor rates are significantly lower than dealership pricing. You get the same expertise and equipment without the dealership markup — and your manufacturer warranty is protected under federal law.

Do you program Audi keys and fobs?

Yes — we program Audi keys, key fobs, and proximity keys for all models. Audi key programming requires specialized equipment that communicates with the immobilizer system at the manufacturer level.
